How the calculation works

Pearson correlationr = Σ[(x − x̄)(y − ȳ)] ÷ √(Σ(x − x̄)² · Σ(y − ȳ)²)
Coefficient of determinationR² = r²
Regression slopeb₁ = Σ[(x − x̄)(y − ȳ)] ÷ Σ(x − x̄)²
Regression lineŷ = b₀ + b₁x

Correlation does not prove causation. Small samples should be interpreted as exploratory.
